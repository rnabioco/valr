Map signals over intervals.

Usage

bed_map(x, y, ...)

concat(.data, sep = ",")

values_unique(.data, sep = ",")

values(.data, sep = ",")

first(.data)

last(.data)

Arguments

x
tbl of intervals
y
tbl of signals
...
name-value pairs of summary functions like min(), count(), concat().

Value

data_frame

Description

Multiple mapping functions can be specified.

Details

Variables from the `x` and `y` tables should be used with `.x` and `.y` suffixes.

Existing grouping variables are maintained in the output, but have `.x` or `.y` suffixes`.

Note

Column names have .x and .y suffixes.

Examples

x <- tibble::frame_data(
 ~chrom, ~start, ~end,
 "chr1", 100, 250,
 "chr2", 250, 500)

y <- tibble::frame_data(
 ~chrom, ~start, ~end, ~value,
 "chr1", 100, 250, 10,
 "chr1", 150, 250, 20,
 "chr2", 250, 500, 500)

# mean, median, sd etc
bed_map(x, y, sum = sum(value.y))


# A tibble: 2 × 4
  chrom start.x end.x   sum
  <chr>   <dbl> <dbl> <dbl>
1  chr1     100   250    30
2  chr2     250   500   500



bed_map(x, y, min = min(value.y), max = max(value.y))


# A tibble: 2 × 5
  chrom start.x end.x   min   max
  <chr>   <dbl> <dbl> <dbl> <dbl>
1  chr1     100   250    10    20
2  chr2     250   500   500   500
